Route logic

Why the line matters

Emberline turns shift spillover into action. The line gets harder when you treat every interruption as an emergency. It gets calmer when you filter, tag duplicates, and keep your working room intact.

Hazards
Barrier stacks need a jump. Low beams need a slide. False-alarm drones must be tagged before they crowd the lane. Missing a packet hurts score more than pride.
Steady windows
Holding steady slows the world and lets load bleed off, but it costs focus. Use it to recover the route, not to hide in it.
Checkpoint calls
Twice per run, the line pauses and asks for one bounded routing decision. Those choices matter because action without judgment is just expensive flailing.
